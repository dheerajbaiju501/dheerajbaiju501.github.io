This work presents a comprehensive survey of multimodal AI agents, examining how large multimodal models enable systems to perceive, reason, plan, and act across modalities such as text, images, audio, and video. It introduces a modality-centric taxonomy for agentic frameworks, analyzes key architectural and reasoning paradigms, and reviews applications ranging from robotics and GUI navigation to multimedia generation and long-video understanding, while also highlighting the scalability and efficiency challenges toward building more capable general-purpose AI systems.
